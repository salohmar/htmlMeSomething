Watercolor is my Therapy

First Things First

I received a watercolor paint set for Christmas in 2019 from my Mother-in-Law. I had never used the medium before and my previous painting experience was Painting with a Twist. I felt bad leaving it unused, so I opened it up, pulled up a Youtube tutorial, and got to work. I absolutely loved it!

Branching Out

Youtube for Good!

I typically avoid all things Youtube. If I don't, I get sucked in for hours and the next thing I know, I've watched 2 hours of makeup tutorials and 3 hours of kids meeting characters at Disney World. I quickly realized that when it came to watercolor, Youtube was going to be my friend. With something so hands on, it really did help to go step by step with someone else. My favorite channel is Let's Make Art. They put out a new project every week and even have a monthly subscription box that sends you all the supplies you'll need for that months projects. What You Need to Get your Paint On

There are a ton of watercolor supplies out there. Every video/class/book, has different recommendations. So here is what I've liked so far:

Brushes to Hold your Paint

I recommend rounds in sizes 2, 6, and 8. I have also found a large flat wash brush super useful when painting landscapes.

  1. Princeton Heritage Series
  2. Princeton Neptune Series

Paint to Put on your Paper

I use both liquid and tube watercolor paints. I have found that student grade are cheaper and work just fine for my purposes!

  1. Dr. Ph Martin Liquid Watercolors
  2. Winsor Newton Tubes

Paper to Absorb your Imagination

You have two options when picking watercolor paper, cold press and hot press. Hot press is a much smoother paper so the water moves around a lot with this. I prefer cold press because the paper is rougher and tends to hold the water in place a little better. This all comes down to what your needs and preferences are. I'm a little particular so I appreciate the control that cold press gives me.

As far as sizes go, it is all up to you and what you are wanting to paint. I like to have a wide variety. I also like having cheaper paper from Target or Amazon to practice on before commiting to using my expensive paper.

  1. Fluid 100 Cold Press
  2. Canson Cold Press

Parting Words & Wisdom
